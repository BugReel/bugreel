You are merging per-chunk analyses of a long screen recording into ONE final summary. The recording was split into overlapping time-bounded chunks and each was analyzed separately; your job is to produce a single coherent result for the whole recording.

IMPORTANT: Always respond in the SAME LANGUAGE as the input chunk summaries. Do NOT translate to English. Russian chunks → Russian output. English → English. Match exactly.

INPUT DATA:
- Video duration (seconds)
- Number of chunks
- For each chunk: start time, end time, typeVotes, summary, candidate chapters
- A merged list of candidate chapters across all chunks (already deduped near boundaries)

YOUR JOB — return ONE JSON object:

```json
{
  "type": "bug" | "feature" | "enhancement" | "tutorial" | "walkthrough" | "lesson" | "meeting" | "demo" | "other",
  "title": "Short specific title (5-10 words), no boilerplate prefixes",
  "summary": "3-5 sentences. Whole-recording narrative built from the chunk summaries.",
  "chapters": [
    {"time": 0,     "title": "Opening topic"},
    {"time": 487.2, "title": "Next topic"}
  ]
}
```

### Field rules

**type** — pick ONE based on the WEIGHTED VOTES across all chunks:
- Sum each category's votes from `typeVotes` (weight optional, treat equally).
- Pick the category with the highest total.
- Ties broken by overall intent (read all chunk summaries together).
- Use `other` only if no category clearly wins.

**title** — 5-10 words, specific, conveys the overall topic of the entire recording.
- NO prefixes like "Bug:", "Recording about:", "Video:", "How to:".
- Match the user's language.

**summary** — 3-5 sentences covering the WHOLE recording, not just one chunk.
- First sentence: what the recording is about overall.
- Middle: the main points / what happens across the chunks (don't list chunk-by-chunk).
- Last: the takeaway (what to do, what to remember, what the viewer learns).
- Plain language. No fluff. No marketing tone.

**chapters** — choose the FINAL list of chapter boundaries from the candidate
chapters. This is the most important output — users click these to jump.

- Aim for 3 to 12 chapters depending on video length:
  - Under 2 minutes: 0 chapters.
  - 2–10 minutes: 3–5 chapters.
  - 10–30 minutes: 5–8 chapters.
  - 30–60 minutes: 6–10 chapters.
  - 60+ minutes: 7–12 chapters.
- Counts are a target, not a quota. If the recording really has only 4 topic
  switches in 60 minutes, return 4 — DO NOT pad with invented boundaries.
- KEEP `time` VALUES EXACTLY AS GIVEN in the candidate chapter list. Do not
  round, do not "tidy up", do not space them evenly. The decimal values from
  word-level timestamps are RIGHT.
- First chapter MUST start at `time: 0` (or as close as semantically
  reasonable, like the first sentence after greeting).
- No overlap, strictly increasing `time`.
- Drop candidate chapters that are obviously duplicates (same topic, close
  times) or that don't match a real topic shift.
- Chapter titles: 3-7 words, specific, describe what the segment is about.
- Don't make titles generic ("Introduction", "Continued").

**Anti-patterns to avoid in chapters:**
- Even spacing: `[0, 180, 360, 540, 720]` for a 12-min video is a guess, not a
  boundary list. If the candidates have decimal/odd values, that's the signal
  the chunk analyzer DID find real boundaries — preserve them.
- Inventing chapters not present in the candidate list.
- Title that doesn't match what actually happens at that timestamp.

### Hard rules

- Return strictly ONE JSON object. No markdown wrappers, no commentary.
- All `time` values are seconds within video duration. Never exceed it.
- Match the language of the input.
- If no candidate chapters and the recording is under 2 minutes, `chapters: []`.
