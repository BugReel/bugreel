### Field rules

**typeVotes** — how this chunk's content votes for the OVERALL recording type. Distribute probability mass (totals ≈ 1.0) across the relevant categories. Most chunks will vote heavily for one type.

Categories: `bug` | `feature` | `enhancement` | `tutorial` | `walkthrough` | `lesson` | `meeting` | `demo` | `other`

- `bug` — speaker reports something broken / shows an error.
- `feature` — proposes a new feature.
- `enhancement` — suggests changes to existing feature.
- `tutorial` / `walkthrough` — explains HOW to do something step-by-step.
- `lesson` — teaches a concept (educational).
- `meeting` — multiple speakers OR decisions/action items with team audience.
- `demo` — showcasing for an audience (sales/marketing tone).
- `other` — none above clearly fit.

Example: a clearly-tutorial chunk → `{"tutorial": 0.9, "walkthrough": 0.1}`.
Example: mixed chunk → `{"tutorial": 0.5, "demo": 0.3, "lesson": 0.2}`.

**summary** — 2-3 sentences. What happens in THIS chunk only.
- Lead with the main activity / topic in the chunk.
- Do NOT pretend to summarize the whole recording — you only see one slice.
- No marketing language. Plain, direct.

**chapters** — topic boundaries that START INSIDE THIS CHUNK.
- Only include chapter boundaries where the speaker pivots to a new topic
  WITHIN this chunk's time range.
- `time` is ABSOLUTE seconds (use values from the word-level timestamps array
  as-is — do not offset).
- Find the EXACT word/phrase where the topic pivots and use that word's timestamp.
- Decimal/odd values are GOOD (`482.1`, `690.6`) — they indicate you found a
  specific word, not a guess.
- Anti-pattern: spacing chapters at round intervals (every 60s, every 180s).
  Don't pad with invented boundaries.
- Aim for 1–4 chapters per chunk depending on topic density. If the speaker
  stays on one topic for the whole chunk, return `[]`.
- Chapter titles: 3–7 words, specific. Avoid generic ("Introduction", "Continued").
- DO NOT include a chapter at `time: 0` unless this chunk actually starts at 0.
  The reducer will add the opening chapter.

### Hard rules

- Return strictly ONE JSON object. No markdown wrappers, no commentary.
- All `time` values are ABSOLUTE seconds (same scale as the word-level
  timestamps you were given). Never invent times outside the chunk's range.
- Match the language of the transcript.
- If this chunk has fewer than ~30 words of speech, return:
  `{"typeVotes": {"other": 1.0}, "summary": "", "chapters": []}`.
